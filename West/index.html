‚ö†Ô∏è Dairy Hill = Primary Traditional Ice Cream Competitor

Dairy Hill Ice Cream is the dominant traditional ice cream establishment in West Linn, but does NOT offer soft serve. Locally-owned with multiple Portland-area locations, strong community presence (fundraising, local events), offers vegan options, and carries trusted Umpqua brand. They are an established destination for families with significant brand loyalty.

Mitigation Strategy: Fast Times should NOT attempt to compete on traditional ice cream quality. Instead, LEAD with soft serve as the primary differentiator (zero competition in this category), emphasize the COMBINED offering (pizza + soft serve = one stop), and INTEGRATE soft serve into the full dining experience. Position as the soft serve destination that happens to also serve excellent pizza, not an ice cream shop trying to compete with Dairy Hill.

  • Pizza Pricing Strategy:
    • 14" Pizza: $20-24 - Position between Bellagio's ($18) and J. Willy's artisan ($32), aligned with Papa Murphy's Family ($20) and Spectator ($22.75-$32.95)
    • 16" Pizza: $24-28 - Between Bellagio's XL ($22) and Elden's premium ($26-37), competitive with Spectator ($27.75-$37.95)

  • Pizza by Slice: $4.50-5.50 - Slightly above Spectator's cheese ($4.25) but competitive with their specialty ($5.25). Enables sampling and appeals to solo diners.
  • Soft Serve Pricing: $2.99-4.99 - Price competitively as ONLY soft serve destination. Consider:
    • Small cone: $2.99-3.49 (competitive with Mike's $1.95 but reflects soft serve premium)
    • Medium cone/cup: $3.99-4.49
    • Large cup: $4.99-5.99
    • Position below traditional ice cream scoops ($4-5) to encourage trial and emphasize value
  • Combo Specials: $26-32 - Create bundles that beat two-stop dining:
    • 14" Pizza + 2 Soft Serves: $28 (vs. $38 separate: $20 pizza + $9 ice cream at Dairy Hill)
    • 16" Pizza + 4 Soft Serves: $32 (family value bundle)
  • Plant-Based Premium: None - Plant-based should be standard offering at same price, not upcharge. Differentiate on values, not price.
  • Patio Seating: Included - Market as premium experience included, not separate upcharge.
  • Competitive Positioning: Mid-premium range - more expensive than take-n-bake Papa Murphy's and budget Bellagio's, but significantly less than J. Willy's artisan ($32) and Elden's premium ($26-37).
